Get paid to research and write technical posts

By Phil Eaton

I'm looking for college students (probably majoring in CS/EE or a related field) interested in researching the structure and history of open-source projects. You will work closely with me to pick a topic and get paid $250 per article on completion. Most blog posts take me 3-6 hours to write which would put this around $40-$80/hr.

After agreeing on a topic, you will do the primary research and writing. I'll help out with guidance, pointers, and editing. Posts should be in the ballpark of 600-1000 words. But so long as the topic is explored to a high degree of quality, the word length is not of critical importance.

Your name and link will be on the post. Posts will be submitted to popular link aggregators and often end up featured on the front page of sites like Hacker News.

Example topics include:

  • The code structure and design decisions behind Chart.js
  • The history of and differentiation between Apache stream processing projects like Flume, Spark, Storm, etc.
  • Writing a PostgreSQL storage extension to SELECT from CSV

You can find more examples of existing posts on the DataStation blog and on my personal blog.

If this sounds interesting, please email me with one or more samples of writing on a technical topic (could be from a school paper you wrote, your own blog, etc.).
